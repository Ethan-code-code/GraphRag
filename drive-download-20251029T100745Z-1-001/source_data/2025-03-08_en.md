- And upon the Amiri Order issued on 2 Dhul-Qa'dah 1445 H, corresponding to 2024/5/10 G.
- And upon the Penal Code Law No. 16 of 1960 and its amendments.
- And upon the Criminal Trials and Procedures Law No. 17 of 1960 and its amendments.
- And upon the Prison Regulation Law No. 26 of 1962.
- And based on the requirements of the public interest.

Decided

## Article (1)
A committee shall be formed, named the (Committee for Monitoring the Status of Prisoners Sentenced to Life Imprisonment), and shall be composed of:

1. A representative from the Public Prosecution, with a rank no less than Attorney General - Chairman
2. A member from the Public Prosecution - Member
3. A member from the Public Prosecution - Member
4. The Director General of the General Department of Preventive Security, or their representative - Member
5. The Director General of the General Department of Criminal Investigation, or their representative - Member
6. The Director General of the General Department of Correctional Institutions, or their representative - Member
7. The Director General of the General Department of Sentence Enforcement, or their representative - Member

## Article (2)
The Committee shall undertake the following competencies:

1. Examining the files of those sentenced to life imprisonment who have served at least (20) years of their sentence, and determining their eligibility for release if the conditions stipulated in the following articles are met.
2. The Committee shall examine the files of those sentenced to life imprisonment and submit a report on their status to both His Excellency the Minister of Interior and His Excellency the Counselor, the Public Prosecutor.

### Article (3)
The release of those sentenced to life imprisonment who have served at least (20) years in prison is subject to the following conditions:
1. There must be reason to believe in the convicted person's self-reformation.
2. They must have demonstrated good conduct and behavior while in prison.
3. Their release must not pose a threat to public security requirements.

### Article (4)
The Public Prosecutor <orig>النائب العام</orig> may order the released person to be placed under electronic monitoring (electronic bracelet) for a period not exceeding five years, determine the conditions to which the released person must adhere, and notify them that any violation of these conditions will be grounds for revoking the release.

### Article (5)
The Honorable Public Prosecutor <orig>السيد النائب العام</orig> shall issue conditional release decisions for those sentenced to life imprisonment whom the committee has deemed eligible for release.

### Article (6)
This decision shall take effect from the date of its issuance and shall be published in the Official Gazette.
